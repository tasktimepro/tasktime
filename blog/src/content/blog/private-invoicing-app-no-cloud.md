Take a look at your last invoice. It probably has your client's full name and address, a detailed breakdown of work performed, your hourly rate or project fee, and your bank details or payment link.

Now ask yourself: does all of that need to live on someone else's server?

## The cloud-first default

Most invoicing apps are cloud-first by design. You create an account, enter your business details, add clients, and start invoicing. Everything gets stored on their servers from the moment you sign up.

This works — until it doesn't. Cloud-first invoicing means:

- **A company you don't control holds your financial data.** Client names, rates, payment history — it's all in their database.
- **You need internet to access your own invoices.** Flaky Wi-Fi at a coffee shop? Good luck pulling up that invoice your client just asked about.
- **You're locked in.** Exporting your data is usually possible but rarely easy. Most people never bother, which is exactly the point.
- **Pricing can change.** The plan you signed up for isn't the plan you'll have in two years.

For a solo freelancer sending a few invoices a month, this is a lot of overhead and risk for a pretty simple need.

## What offline-capable invoicing looks like

An invoicing app that works without the cloud is simpler than it sounds:

- **Your invoices live on your device.** Create them, edit them, export them — all without an internet connection.
- **PDF export works locally.** You generate the PDF in your browser, not on a remote server.
- **No account means no data on their end.** If there's no sign-up, there's nothing to store about you.
- **Sync is opt-in, on your terms.** If you *want* backup or multi-device access, you decide where the data goes.

## How TaskTime Pro approaches this

TaskTime Pro is a PWA — a progressive web app — that runs entirely in your browser. Your invoices, client records, and business info are stored locally. The app works offline, so you can create and review invoices without connectivity.

When you generate an invoice, the PDF is created right in your browser. No server round-trip, no upload. The file goes from your device to wherever you choose to save it.

If you want your invoicing data available on another device, you can connect Google Drive for sync. Sync documents are stored in the app-data area of your own Drive rather than a TaskTime-hosted workspace.

There's no account to create. No monthly fee. No server holding your billing history. You control your financial records completely, and you can export everything at any time.

## It's a mindset shift

Once you've used a private invoicing app, going back to cloud-first feels strange. You start wondering why you ever needed a company to hold your invoices for you in the first place.

The answer, usually, is convenience. But when the private option is just as convenient — and it is — there's no trade-off left.
