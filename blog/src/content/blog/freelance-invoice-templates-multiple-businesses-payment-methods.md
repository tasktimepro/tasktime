Sending an invoice should not feel like rebuilding the same document from scratch every month.

Most freelancers need the same pieces again and again: a business name, logo, invoice number, due date, tax details, bank details, PayPal address, project work, and payment instructions. The details change, but the structure rarely does.

That is why TaskTime separates invoice templates, business profiles, and payment methods. Each one handles a different part of the invoice, so you can reuse the right setup without copying old PDFs or editing a spreadsheet.

## What invoice templates are for

An invoice template controls how the invoice behaves and looks.

In TaskTime, templates can define:

- Invoice number format
- Sequential numbering
- Due date rules
- Layout style
- Header alignment
- Whether to show the billing period
- Whether to show the project title
- Whether to show the business logo
- Whether to use the business primary color

That makes templates useful for more than visual polish. A good custom invoice template also reduces mistakes.

For example, if one type of client always gets 30-day payment terms and another needs a simpler invoice layout, you can create separate templates instead of changing the same fields manually every time.

## Why multiple business profiles matter

Many freelancers do not work under one simple identity forever.

You might have:

- A personal freelance name
- A registered company
- A side business with separate branding
- Different VAT or tax details
- Separate contact details for different types of work

TaskTime lets you save multiple business profiles, then pick the right one when creating an invoice. Each business profile can include sender details such as business name, address, registration number, VAT number, tax number, email, phone, custom fields, tax defaults, logo, and primary brand color.

That matters because invoices are legal and financial records. If you operate under more than one business identity, the invoice should show the correct sender details every time.

It also keeps the workflow cleaner. You do not need separate invoicing apps just because you have separate business names.

## Why reusable payment methods save time

Payment details are another place where copy-paste errors can get expensive.

TaskTime payment methods let you save reusable instructions such as:

- Full account name
- Bank name
- IBAN
- SWIFT/BIC
- Bank address
- PayPal email
- Custom payment details

You can keep a default payment method for normal invoices and create additional ones for special cases. For example, you might use bank transfer for local clients, PayPal for international clients, or a custom field for a payment reference instruction.

Clear payment instructions help clients pay faster because they do not have to search an email thread for your bank details.

## The advantage of keeping the pieces separate

The useful part is not just that TaskTime has templates, businesses, and payment methods. It is that they are separate reusable pieces.

That gives you combinations like:

- One business profile with two invoice templates
- Two business profiles using the same payment method
- One template used across several clients
- A special payment method for one region or client type
- A clean branded invoice without re-entering branding every time

This is much better than saving one old invoice as a starting point. Old invoices contain old client details, old totals, and old dates. Reusing them invites mistakes.

Reusable settings give you the speed of copying without the risk of copying the wrong thing.

## Branding without making invoicing complicated

Freelancers often search for "branded invoice template" because they want invoices that look professional without needing a design tool.

TaskTime keeps branding simple:

- Add a logo to your business profile
- Choose a primary color
- Decide in the invoice template whether to show the logo
- Decide whether the template should use the business color
- Preview the result before sending or downloading

The invoice still stays practical. Branding should make the document look trustworthy, not turn it into a brochure.

## Better defaults mean fewer invoice mistakes

The biggest benefit of reusable invoice settings is consistency.

When your defaults are set:

- Invoice numbers follow the same pattern
- Payment terms are applied consistently
- Business details stay accurate
- Payment instructions are not forgotten
- Tax details are tied to the right business profile
- The invoice layout stays predictable

That saves a few minutes per invoice, but the bigger win is avoiding errors that cause payment delays.

Clients should not have to ask where to send payment, which business issued the invoice, or whether the due date is correct.

## A good setup for most freelancers

If you are just getting started, keep it simple:

1. Create your main business profile.
2. Add your logo, primary color, tax details, and contact information.
3. Create one default payment method.
4. Create one standard invoice template with your normal due date.
5. Preview the sample invoice before using it with a client.

You can add more templates, businesses, and payment methods later when you actually need them.

The goal is not to build a complex invoicing system. The goal is to make each invoice faster, clearer, and less error-prone.
