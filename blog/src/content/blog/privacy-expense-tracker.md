As a freelancer, you've got expenses coming from everywhere: software subscriptions, domain renewals, a co-working day pass, that client lunch. Keeping track of it all matters — especially at tax time or when a client asks for a cost breakdown.

But here's the thing: most expense trackers want you to create an account, connect your bank, and store everything on their servers. That's convenient, sure. But it also means a third party has a very detailed picture of how you spend money.

## What a good expense tracker actually needs

Forget the AI-powered receipt scanning and the 47 integrations. For most freelancers, expense tracking comes down to a few things:

- **Quick entry.** You should be able to log an expense in under 10 seconds. Name, amount, date, which project it belongs to — done.
- **Recurring expenses.** Things like monthly software subscriptions shouldn't need to be entered manually every time.
- **Billable vs. non-billable.** You need to know which expenses to pass through to clients and which are your own overhead.
- **Reliable export.** At minimum, you should be able to get your expenses out as a file you can hand to your accountant.

## How TaskTime Pro keeps it simple and private

In TaskTime Pro, expenses live right alongside your projects, tasks, and invoices. You log an expense, tag it to a project, mark it as billable or not, and move on. Recurring expenses get created automatically.

The key difference: your expense records stay in your browser by default. There is no account required or TaskTime-hosted workspace database. If you enable Google Drive sync, sync documents are stored in the app-data area of your own Drive rather than a TaskTime-hosted workspace.

## A quick tip for staying organized

Create a habit of logging expenses the same day they happen. It takes a few seconds in the moment and saves you hours of receipt archaeology later. If an expense is billable, tag it to the right project immediately — your future self will thank you when it's time to invoice.
