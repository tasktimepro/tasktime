Here's the usual path for freelancers trying to track expenses: you look up "expense tracker," find a tool designed for mid-size companies, create an account, skip the bank connection prompt, ignore the receipt scanning feature, and finally — *finally* — log that $12 domain renewal.

It shouldn't take more effort to record an expense than to make the purchase.

## Why most expense trackers are overkill

The majority of expense tracking tools are built for businesses with employees, corporate cards, and accounting departments. They offer:

- Bank account connections you don't need
- Receipt OCR scanning that misreads half your receipts
- Approval workflows for a team of one
- Monthly fees for the privilege of all the above

As a freelancer, you're probably tracking software subscriptions, the occasional client lunch, travel costs, and project-specific purchases. You need a list, some categories, and a way to tie expenses to projects. That's about it.

## What a free expense tracker should do

The bar is honestly pretty low:

- **Quick entry.** Amount, description, date, project. Ten seconds, tops.
- **Categories.** Group expenses so you can make sense of them later — software, travel, supplies, whatever fits your work.
- **Billable tagging.** Know which expenses to pass through to clients and which are your own cost of doing business.
- **Recurring expenses.** That monthly Figma subscription shouldn't need to be entered by hand every month.
- **No subscription.** An expense tracker that charges you a monthly fee to track expenses is a bit ironic, isn't it?

## How TaskTime Pro handles expenses

## A simpler approach to tax time

One of the best things about keeping expenses organized throughout the year is that tax season stops being stressful. When your accountant asks for a breakdown of business expenses, you can export your data and hand it over — instead of digging through bank statements and email receipts in a panic.

The trick is making it easy enough that you actually do it consistently. If logging an expense takes more than a few seconds, you'll skip it. If it takes two taps, you won't.
