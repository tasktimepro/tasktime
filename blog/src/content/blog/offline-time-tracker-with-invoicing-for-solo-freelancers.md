We all know we *should* track our time. But for a lot of freelancers, it's one of those things that starts strong on Monday and fizzles out by Wednesday.

The problem isn't laziness — it's usually that the workflow is just annoying enough to skip. Here are five tips that actually make time tracking stick.

## 1. Track against real tasks, not vague labels

"Design work" tells you nothing two weeks later. Instead, track time against specific tasks like "Redesign homepage hero section" or "Write blog post draft for Client X."

In TaskTime Pro, timers are tied directly to tasks within projects. When you look back at your week, you can see exactly what you worked on and how long each piece took — which makes invoicing a breeze.

## 2. Use timers for active work, manual entries for the rest

Not everything fits neatly into a start-stop timer. Maybe you spent 45 minutes on a call and forgot to start the timer. No problem — just add a manual time entry after the fact.

The best time-tracking workflow uses both: timers when you're heads-down, and manual entries to fill in the gaps. TaskTime Pro supports both equally, so you're never stuck.

## 3. Don't wait until Friday to review your week

Quick daily check: did today's tracked time actually reflect what you worked on? Two minutes at the end of the day saves you from staring at a confusing timesheet on invoice day.

## 4. Keep your time tracker open (or in your face)

The number one reason people forget to track time is that the tool is buried in a tab somewhere. TaskTime Pro works as a PWA, which means you can install it and keep it in your dock or home screen.

Out of sight, out of mind. Keep it visible.

## 5. Go straight from time entries to invoices

The best part of good time tracking is a painless invoice. If your tracked time lives in the same tool as your invoices, you don't need to copy hours into a spreadsheet or a separate billing app.

In TaskTime Pro, you create an invoice from a project, and the tracked time is right there. No exporting, no re-entering, no guessing.
