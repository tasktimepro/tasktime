Freelancing across countries is normal now. The accounting side is where it gets annoying.

One client pays in USD, another in EUR, another in GBP, and a recurring software expense lands in CHF. Individually, each invoice or expense is easy to understand. Together, they make reports harder to read.

If you want to know how the month is going, you do not want a mental math exercise. You want a clear total in your preferred currency, with the option to inspect the original currencies when needed.

That is what TaskTime Pro's multi-currency reporting is designed for.

## The problem with source currencies

Source currency is the original currency of the record.

For example:

- A US client invoice in USD
- A UK client invoice in GBP
- A eurozone expense in EUR
- A Swiss software subscription in CHF

Source currencies are important. They reflect what the client saw, what the invoice said, and what the expense actually cost.

But source currencies are not enough for overview reporting. A monthly report that says "USD 2,000 + EUR 1,400 + GBP 900" is accurate, but it does not immediately answer the bigger question: how much did I make in my own reporting currency?

## Preferred currency gives you the overview

TaskTime Pro lets you set a preferred currency.

Reports can then show totals in that preferred currency when exchange rates are available. That gives you a cleaner view of:

- Revenue issued
- Payments received
- Outstanding invoices
- Overdue invoices
- Expenses
- Estimated profit
- Output tax
- Uninvoiced expenses

This is especially useful in the Overview and Monthly Summary reports, where you want fast answers rather than a currency-by-currency ledger.

For searches like "multi-currency reports" or "multi-currency accounting reports," this is the feature most freelancers actually need: keep the original currencies, but make totals understandable.

## Source-currency mode is still available

Preferred-currency totals are useful, but they should not hide the original records.

TaskTime Pro reports include a currency display option:

- Preferred currency
- Source currencies

Preferred currency is best for summaries and comparisons. Source currencies are best when you want to review the original invoice or expense amounts.

That distinction matters. Currency conversion helps you understand performance, but your invoice records should still preserve what was actually sent to the client.

## Where conversion appears

Multi-currency handling appears across the places where freelancers usually need totals:

- Dashboard overviews
- Report summary cards
- Monthly report lines
- Invoice reports
- Outstanding invoice reports
- Client statement summaries
- Expense summaries
- Uninvoiced work summaries

The goal is consistency. If you are looking at paid revenue, outstanding balances, or filtered expense totals, TaskTime Pro tries to present the information in the currency view you selected.

## Why payment snapshots matter

Exchange rates move.

If a USD invoice was paid weeks ago and your preferred currency is EUR, the converted value should not drift every time you open the dashboard. For paid invoices and paid expenses, TaskTime Pro can use captured payment conversion details where available.

That helps reports reflect the payment context more closely instead of constantly recalculating old paid items with today's rate.

For unpaid or current reporting totals, live or cached exchange rates can still help estimate the preferred-currency value.

## What happens when rates are unavailable

Currency conversion should fail carefully.

If exchange rates are unavailable, TaskTime Pro warns you and falls back to original amounts where needed. That is better than silently showing a total that looks precise but is missing a conversion.

This can happen if:

- You are offline
- The rate provider cannot be reached
- A needed currency is missing from the available rate data
- Only stale cached rates are available

In those cases, source-currency display is still useful. You can see the original amounts and continue reviewing the report without losing access to your records.

## Reports are estimates, not tax advice

Multi-currency reporting is extremely useful for business visibility, but it is still a reporting aid.

Taxes, VAT, exchange-rate rules, and accounting treatment can depend on your country, payment date, invoice date, accounting basis, and local rules. TaskTime Pro gives you organized records and estimated converted totals. It does not replace professional tax or accounting advice.

That is why the source records remain visible and exports stay useful. Your accountant can review the original invoice currencies, dates, totals, and payment details.

## A practical monthly workflow

If you work with international clients, try this once a month:

1. Open Reports.
2. Choose the month or date range.
3. Keep currency display set to Preferred currency for the overview.
4. Review revenue issued, payments received, expenses, and estimated profit.
5. Switch to Source currencies if a total needs inspection.
6. Export the monthly CSV pack or PDF if you need records for bookkeeping.

That gives you both views: the business overview in your own currency and the underlying detail in the original currencies.

## Why this is better than a spreadsheet

You can do currency conversion in a spreadsheet, but the manual work adds up quickly.

You need to copy invoice totals, copy expense totals, choose exchange rates, remember which date basis you used, and avoid overwriting formulas. Then you need to repeat it next month.

TaskTime Pro already has the invoice, expense, client, project, payment, and currency data. Reports use that connected data to give you totals without rebuilding the same spreadsheet every time.

That is the advantage of multi-currency reports for freelancers: not just currency conversion, but conversion connected to the work, invoices, expenses, and payment status you already track.
