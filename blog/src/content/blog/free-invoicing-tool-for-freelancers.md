Invoicing should be one of the simplest parts of freelancing: you did the work, now you send a bill. But somehow, most invoicing tools have turned this into a subscription business.

The typical "free plan" for invoicing looks something like this:

- Up to 5 invoices per month (need more? $15/month)
- Their logo on your invoices (remove it? $15/month)
- Basic templates only (custom templates? $15/month)
- No recurring invoices on the free tier

You get the idea. "Free" is the demo. The real product costs money.

## What a free invoicing tool should actually do

If you're a freelancer sending invoices to clients, your needs are probably straightforward:

- **Create invoices from your tracked time and expenses.** You already did the work — the tool should pull the data in, not make you re-enter it.
- **Customize the look.** Your invoice should have your business name, logo, and layout — not the tool's branding stamped across the top.
- **Export to PDF.** You need to email invoices or save them for records. PDF is the standard.
- **No artificial limits.** Whether you send two invoices a month or twenty, the tool should work the same way.
- **Track payment status.** Mark invoices as paid, partially paid, or outstanding. Basic, but important.
