You've probably heard the term "local-first" popping up more lately. It sounds technical, but the idea is simple: your data lives on your device first, and the cloud is optional.

Compare that to most SaaS tools, where your data lives on *their* servers first, and you get to access it through *their* app as long as you keep paying.

## What changes when your tools are local-first

Here's what day-to-day freelancing looks like with a local-first approach:

- **No loading spinners.** Your data is already on your device. Opening the app is instant, even without internet.
- **No outage anxiety.** When Toggl or FreshBooks goes down, you're stuck. When your tool is local-first, you barely notice.
- **No vendor lock-in.** Your records aren't trapped in someone else's database. You can export, back up, or walk away whenever you want.
- **No surprise pricing changes.** Even if the tool changes its model, your existing data is on your machine.

## The sync question

"But what if I use two devices?" — totally fair question.

Local-first doesn't mean local-only. TaskTime, for example, lets you sync across devices through your own Google Drive. The data goes from your browser to your Drive account — we never touch it or even see it.

So you get the convenience of sync without the privacy trade-off of storing everything on a company's servers.

## Try it and feel the difference

If you've been using cloud-first tools your whole freelance career, switching to something local-first feels surprisingly good. Everything is faster. You stop worrying about whether you saved. And you realize how much of the "product" in most SaaS tools is really just managing the connection to their servers.
