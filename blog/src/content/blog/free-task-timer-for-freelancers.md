You'd think finding a free task timer would be straightforward. You search for one, click the first result, and... you're staring at a sign-up form.

Then you create the account, start timing a task, and realize the free plan only tracks three projects. Or it doesn't export. Or the timer data disappears after 30 days unless you upgrade.

This is the "free tier" game, and almost every time-tracking app plays it.

## What "free" should actually mean

A genuinely free task timer should let you:

- **Start timing immediately.** No account, no email, no onboarding wizard. You open it and go.
- **Track unlimited tasks and projects.** If there's a cap on how many things you can time, it's a trial — not a free tool.
- **Keep your data.** Your tracked time shouldn't vanish because you didn't upgrade by some deadline.
- **Work offline.** A timer that needs a constant internet connection is a server dependency, not a utility.

That's a pretty low bar, but you'd be surprised how few tools actually clear it.

## Why freelancers need a task-level timer

A basic stopwatch app can technically track time. But if you're freelancing, you need more than that. You need to know *what* you spent time on, not just *how long* you worked.

Task-level timing means every time entry is tied to a specific piece of work: "Write proposal for Client X" or "Fix navigation bug on landing page." When it's time to invoice, you're not guessing — you've got a clean record of what happened and how long it took.
