Here's a setup that might sound familiar: you plan tasks in one app, track time in another, log expenses somewhere else, and create invoices in a fourth tool. Every tool has its own login, its own data silo, and its own monthly charge.

It works — kind of. But every time you switch between tools, you're losing context and spending time on admin that doesn't earn you money.

## The case for a unified workflow

When your tasks, time tracking, expenses, and invoicing all live in the same tool:

- **Starting a timer is one click.** You're looking at a task, you click start. No switching tabs.
- **Your timesheet builds itself.** Tracked time is automatically connected to the right project and task.
- **Invoicing is already half done.** When it's time to bill, the hours and expenses are already there — no manual data entry.
- **You get a real picture of your week.** A dashboard that pulls from tasks, time, and projects shows you what actually happened, not what you remember.

## The privacy angle

Here's something people don't think about: if you use four different SaaS tools, four different companies have a piece of your work data. Your task titles (which often contain client names), your work patterns, your billing rates, your client list — scattered across vendors.

With a local-first tool like TaskTime Pro, all of that stays in your browser. One place, under your control. If you add sync, it goes to *your* Google Drive — not someone else's servers.

## What this looks like in practice

A typical day with TaskTime Pro:

1. Check your planner to see what's up today
2. Start a timer on the first task
3. Work. Pause or stop the timer when you're done.
4. Log a quick expense if one came up
5. At the end of the week, generate an invoice with everything already attached

No copy-pasting between apps. No "wait, how many hours did I work on that project?" moments. Just a clean flow from planning to getting paid.
