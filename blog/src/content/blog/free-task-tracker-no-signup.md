There's a weird pattern with task trackers: you search for a free one, click through, and the first thing you see is a registration form asking for your name, email, and company size.

You just wanted to organize some tasks. Now you're being onboarded.

## The problem with "free" task trackers

Most free task trackers come with strings attached:

- **Limited projects.** Free means three projects. Four? That's the Pro plan.
- **Limited collaborators.** Even if you work solo, some tools gate features behind "team" tiers.
- **Cloud-only storage.** Your task data lives on their servers from the start. Delete your account and it's gone.
- **Upsell pressure.** Every other click is a nudge to upgrade. The free tier exists to make you feel like you're missing out.

None of this is necessarily evil — companies need to make money. But if you're a freelancer who just needs a clean task list tied to your projects, it's overkill.

## What a good free task tracker actually looks like

For most freelancers, the requirements are pretty simple:

- **Projects and tasks.** Group your work by project, break it down into tasks, maybe add a few subtasks. That's the core.
- **No time limit.** Your task data shouldn't expire because you stopped paying.
- **Fast entry.** Adding a task should take seconds, not clicks through a modal wizard.
- **Works right away.** Open the app, start adding tasks. No account creation, no email verification, no waiting for a confirmation link.

## How TaskTime Pro does it

TaskTime Pro is a task tracker that lives in your browser. You open it, create a project, and start adding tasks. That's it — no account, no sign-up, no email.

Your tasks support subtasks, so you can break bigger deliverables into manageable pieces. Each task can have a timer attached, and when you complete work, the time entries stay connected to the right task and project.
