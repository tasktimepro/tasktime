A monthly freelance report should answer simple questions.

What did I invoice? What actually got paid? What did I spend? What is still overdue? How many hours did I work? What billable work has not been invoiced yet?

Those questions sound basic, but many freelancers still answer them with a spreadsheet assembled from invoices, bank records, timesheets, and memory.

TaskTime reports are meant to make that monthly review easier.

## Start with the overview

The Reports overview gives you summary cards for the selected period.

You can review:

- Issued revenue
- Received payments
- Outstanding invoices
- Overdue invoices
- Expenses
- Estimated profit
- Hours worked
- Uninvoiced work
- Output tax
- Billable utilization

That gives you a fast read on the month before you export anything.

For freelancers searching for a "freelance dashboard" or "monthly freelance report," this is usually the missing piece: not a complex accounting system, just a clean view of the records that already exist.

## Separate invoiced revenue from paid revenue

One of the most useful parts of monthly reporting is separating issued invoices from received payments.

Issued revenue answers:

What did I bill this month?

Received payments answer:

What money actually came in this month?

Both matter. If you invoice a lot but payments are delayed, the month can look good on paper while cash flow is tight. If old invoices were paid this month, cash flow may look strong even if new work slowed down.

TaskTime lets you filter income by invoice date or paid date so you can review the month from the angle that matters.

## Review expenses while they are still fresh

Expenses are easiest to fix while you still remember them.

In TaskTime, monthly reports can include expenses with supplier, category, project, client, payment status, billing status, tax amount, and claim status.

That helps you catch problems early:

- Missing supplier names
- Uncategorized expenses
- Paid expenses without paid dates
- Billable expenses not attached to invoices
- Tax details that need review
- Expenses that should be marked claimed or unclaimed

Waiting until tax season makes all of this harder. A monthly expense review turns it into a short habit.

## Use estimated profit as a practical signal

Estimated profit in TaskTime is a reporting estimate based on paid revenue minus filtered expenses.

It is not a full accounting profit and loss statement. It does not replace tax advice or formal bookkeeping. But it is still useful.

It gives you a practical signal:

- Did income cover expenses this month?
- Are expenses rising faster than revenue?
- Did a big annual subscription distort the month?
- Is cash flow improving or tightening?

For solo freelancers, that is often enough to make better decisions before the end of the quarter.

## Check billable utilization

Revenue is only part of the story. Time matters too.

TaskTime monthly reports can show total hours and billable utilization. That helps answer:

- How much did I work?
- How much of that time was billable?
- Did admin take over the month?
- Are low-billable weeks becoming a pattern?

This is useful even if you do not bill hourly. Billable utilization shows whether your working time is going toward client delivery or getting absorbed by overhead.

## Find uninvoiced work before it disappears

Missed billable work is one of the easiest ways freelancers lose money.

TaskTime reports can show uninvoiced work: billable hours and billable expenses that exist in your records but are not yet on an invoice.

That gives you a monthly safety check.

Before closing the month, review the uninvoiced work total. If something should be billed, create the invoice while the details are still fresh. If something should not be billed, update the task or expense so the report stays clean.

## Export what you need

Once the month looks right, exports are there for bookkeeping.

TaskTime supports monthly report exports such as:

- Monthly summary CSV
- Monthly invoices CSV
- Monthly expenses CSV
- Monthly hours CSV
- Monthly tax summary CSV
- Review checklist CSV
- Monthly report PDF
- Accountant-friendly export packs

The point is not to create pretty files. The point is to create boring, useful records that can be opened in a spreadsheet or shared with an accountant.

## A simple monthly routine

Here is a practical end-of-month flow:

1. Open Reports.
2. Choose Last Month or a custom date range.
3. Review summary cards.
4. Check issued revenue and paid revenue separately.
5. Review outstanding and overdue invoices.
6. Clean up expense categories and tax details.
7. Review uninvoiced work.
8. Export the monthly pack if needed.

That should take minutes, not hours, if the work was tracked throughout the month.

## Why connected reports are easier

A spreadsheet can calculate totals, but it does not know your workflow.

TaskTime reports know how your records connect:

- Time entries belong to tasks
- Tasks belong to projects
- Projects belong to clients
- Expenses can belong to clients or projects
- Invoices include time and expenses
- Payment status stays with the invoice

That means the monthly report is not a separate admin project. It is a view of the work you already recorded.
