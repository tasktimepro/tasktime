Think about what's inside a typical invoice: your client's name, the project details, how much you charge, when you worked, your bank details or payment link. That's a lot of sensitive information.

Most invoicing tools upload all of that to their servers as soon as you hit save. It works, but it means a company you don't control holds a detailed record of your business relationships.

## The "privacy-first" checklist

Not every tool that mentions privacy actually delivers it. Here's what to look for:

- **Your data stays on your device by default.** If the app needs the internet just to create a basic invoice, it's cloud-first, not privacy-first.
- **Sync is optional, not mandatory.** You should choose when and where your data goes — not have it uploaded automatically to someone else's server.
- **You can export everything.** If you can't get your invoices, clients, and records out in a portable format, you don't really own them.
- **The tool works offline.** This isn't just about airplanes. It's about your workflow not breaking during an outage or a flaky connection.

## How TaskTime Pro handles this

TaskTime Pro keeps your invoicing data in your browser's local storage. Nothing touches our servers — we don't even have a database for user data.

If you want backup and sync across devices, you can connect your own Google Drive. The data goes from your browser to your Drive, encrypted in transit. We never see it.

You can also generate PDF invoices, customize templates, and track payment status — all without creating an account or handing over your data.

## Why it matters for freelancers

When you're freelancing, your client list and billing history *is* your business. Locking it into a SaaS platform means you're always one pricing change or shutdown away from losing access to records you need.

A privacy-first approach flips that. Your data is yours first, and the tool works around that — not the other way around.
