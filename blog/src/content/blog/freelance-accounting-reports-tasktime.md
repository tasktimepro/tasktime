Most freelancers do their accounting in reverse.

You finish the work, send the invoices, pay for tools and subscriptions, track a few expenses, and then once a month or once a year you open a spreadsheet and try to rebuild what happened.

That is the painful part TaskTime reports are designed to remove.

TaskTime already knows your projects, time entries, expenses, invoices, clients, and payment status. Reports turn that data into simple accounting summaries you can understand quickly, export cleanly, and share with your accountant when needed.

It is not trying to replace an accountant or become a full accounting system. It is there to answer the questions freelancers ask all the time: what did I invoice, what was paid, what is still outstanding, what expenses can I claim, and what work still needs to be billed?

## Start with the monthly report

The monthly report is the easiest place to begin. Pick a date range and TaskTime gives you a clear overview of the period:

- Revenue issued
- Revenue received
- Outstanding invoices
- Overdue invoices
- Expenses
- Estimated profit
- Output tax
- Hours worked
- Uninvoiced work
- Billable utilization

That is the basic freelance bookkeeping picture in one place. Instead of opening invoices in one tool, expenses in another, and timesheets in a third, you can review the month from the same app where the work was tracked.

For common searches like "freelance accounting reports" or "monthly report for freelancers," this is the real need: not complicated finance software, just a reliable monthly summary.

## Know what was invoiced and what was paid

Freelance income is not just one number. There is a difference between money you invoiced and money you actually received.

TaskTime keeps those views separate so you can see both clearly:

- Issued invoices show what you billed during the period
- Paid revenue shows what actually came in
- Outstanding invoices show what clients still owe
- Overdue invoices help you spot who needs a reminder

That distinction matters for bookkeeping, tax prep, and cash flow. A great month on paper does not help much if half the invoices are still unpaid.

The invoice report also gives you a clean register of invoice numbers, clients, dates, status, tax, totals, and projects. When your accountant asks for an invoice list, you do not need to rebuild it by hand.

## Review expenses before tax time

Expense tracking is only useful if you can make sense of it later.

TaskTime reports let you review expenses by date, supplier, category, project, client, paid status, tax amount, and claim status. That makes it much easier to spot missing details before tax time instead of finding them when the deadline is already close.

For freelancers, this is where a simple expense report can save real time. You can see software subscriptions, professional services, travel, office costs, and other business expenses in one place, then export the list as a CSV.

If you need to track whether expenses were already claimed for a VAT or tax period, TaskTime supports marking selected expenses as claimed or unclaimed. That helps avoid counting the same expense twice.

## Get a VAT or tax summary without guessing

VAT, sales tax, and tax reporting can get complicated fast, especially when records are incomplete.

TaskTime's VAT / Tax Summary keeps the important pieces visible:

- Sales tax or output VAT from invoices
- Expense tax or input VAT from expenses
- Tax totals grouped by label or rate
- Claim status for expenses
- Missing tax details that need review

The goal is not to file your return for you or give legal tax advice. The goal is to give you an organized worksheet based on the data you already entered, so you can review it yourself or send it to someone qualified.

That is especially useful if you search for things like "VAT report for freelancers," "tax report for freelancers," or "freelancer bookkeeping export." You usually do not need a giant accounting platform. You need clean source records and totals you can check.

## See hours worked and work still waiting to be invoiced

Accounting is not only about money that already moved. For service-based freelancers, time is the source of future invoices.

The Hours Worked report shows logged time by client, project, and task, including billable and non-billable hours. You can also see billable utilization, which helps answer a simple but important question: how much of your working time is actually billable?

TaskTime also shows uninvoiced work. That means billable time and expenses that exist in your records but have not yet been attached to an invoice.

This is one of the most useful reports for freelancers because missed billable work is easy to overlook. If you tracked the task, logged the time, and marked it as billable, TaskTime can help you find it before it slips through the cracks.

## Export an accountant-friendly pack

When it is time to share records, TaskTime can export more than one spreadsheet.

The accountant pack brings the main reporting files together, including invoice data, expenses, time entries, tax summary details, a review checklist, a monthly summary PDF, and invoice PDFs where available.

That makes the handoff much cleaner. Instead of sending scattered files or screenshots, you can give your accountant a structured export built from the same records you use day to day.

CSV exports are intentionally boring: stable columns, plain numbers, ISO-style dates, and source records where possible. That is exactly what spreadsheets and accountants prefer.

## Why this works better than starting from a spreadsheet

Spreadsheets are flexible, but they are usually disconnected from the work.

TaskTime reports work because the data is already connected:

- Time entries belong to tasks
- Tasks belong to projects
- Projects belong to clients
- Expenses can be tied to projects or clients
- Invoices can include tracked time and billable expenses
- Payment status stays with the invoice

By the time you open Reports, most of the bookkeeping structure already exists. You are reviewing and exporting, not rebuilding.

That is the whole point: simple freelance accounting reports from the work you already tracked.

## A simpler way to stay ready

You do not need to wait until tax season to understand your business. A few minutes at the end of each month is enough to review invoices, check outstanding payments, clean up expenses, and export a report if you need one.

TaskTime keeps that workflow lightweight: track the work, log the expenses, send the invoices, then open Reports when you need the accounting view.

No spreadsheet archaeology. No guessing where the numbers came from. Just clear reports you can actually use.
